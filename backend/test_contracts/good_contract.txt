Freelance Development Agreement

Parties:
Client: TechCorp LLC (Commercial Registration No. 1010123456, Riyadh)
Freelancer: Ahmed Ali (National ID: 1234567890, Email: ahmed@email.com)

1. Scope of Work
Freelancer will develop an e-commerce website strictly per the attached Specifications Document (Appendix A).
Any changes to scope require a signed written Change Order. Each approved change order adds to the project fee and timeline.

2. Payment
Total fee: 15,000 SAR.
- 50% deposit (7,500 SAR) due upon contract signing before work begins.
- 50% balance (7,500 SAR) due within 7 days of final delivery acceptance.
Invoices unpaid after 7 days incur a 2% monthly late fee on the outstanding balance.
Freelancer may suspend work if payment is more than 14 days overdue.

3. Timeline
Estimated delivery: 6 weeks from receipt of deposit.
Delays caused by client (late feedback, missing assets) extend the deadline by equivalent time.
Freelancer will provide weekly progress updates.

4. Revisions
Two (2) rounds of revisions included within the agreed scope.
Additional revision rounds: 750 SAR per round, invoiced separately.

5. Intellectual Property
All intellectual property rights transfer to the client upon receipt of full final payment.
Until full payment is received, all rights remain with the Freelancer.
Freelancer retains the right to display the completed work in their portfolio and CV.

6. Confidentiality
Freelancer agrees to keep client business information confidential for 2 years after contract end.
This clause does not prevent Freelancer from listing the client name and project type in their portfolio.

7. Termination
Either party may terminate with 7 days written notice.
Upon termination, client must pay for all work completed to date within 5 business days.
If client terminates without cause after work has begun, a 20% kill fee applies on the remaining balance.

8. Dispute Resolution
Parties agree to attempt good-faith negotiation for 14 days before escalating.
Unresolved disputes will be referred to the Saudi Center for Commercial Arbitration (SCCA), Riyadh.
This agreement is governed by Saudi Civil Transactions Law (نظام المعاملات المدنية).
